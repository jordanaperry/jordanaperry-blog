# About

Hi, I'm Jordan — a systems administrator turned field service engineer, 
working my way back into IT as an IAM Engineer.

I spent nearly a decade in IT at Gracenote and Nielsen, where I managed 
infrastructure for 25,000+ endpoints, administered Okta and Active Directory, 
deployed Tanium across the enterprise, and got my hands dirty with everything 
from helpdesk to systems architecture. I loved it.

A career detour took me into field service engineering at Thermo Fisher 
Scientific, where I install and maintain electron microscopes for research 
labs around the world. It's fascinating work — but my heart is in IT.

So I'm building my way back. This homelab is my proving ground — a place 
to build real skills in Identity and Access Management, network security, 
and infrastructure automation. Everything on this blog is something I 
actually built, broke, fixed, and documented.

## The homelab

The network runs pfSense with full VLAN segmentation, a Proxmox 
hypervisor, self-hosted services, and an ocean/scuba diving naming theme 
because why not make it fun. Everything is documented, version controlled 
in Gitea, and deployed with Terraform.

## The goal

IAM Engineer — specifically targeting Microsoft, Okta, and Google identity 
platforms. SC-900 → Okta Professional → SC-300 → Okta Administrator is 
the certification path.

## Outside of tech

Scuba diver, cyclist, traveler, board game enthusiast. Based in Berkeley, CA.
